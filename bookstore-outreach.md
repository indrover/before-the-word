# Bookstore Outreach Plan — *Before the Word* by Praveen Maloo

---

## Strategy Overview

**Book profile:** Nonfiction — the parallel between ancient Sanskrit philosophy and modern AI/language models. Available as paperback (Barnes & Noble, ISBN 979-8349204081) and Kindle.

**Pitch angle:** The book sits at the intersection of three strong retail categories:
- **AI / Technology** (transformer architecture, LLMs, NLP)
- **Philosophy of Language / Mind** (Pāṇini, Bhartṛhari, Vāk)
- **Eastern Studies / Indian Philosophy** (Sanskrit, Vedic tradition)

Lead with whichever angle fits each store's strongest section.

**Before you pitch:**
- Ensure the paperback is listed through **Ingram Content Group** (IngramSpark). Most US/UK/Australian stores order exclusively through Ingram — being in their catalogue is often more powerful than a cold email.
- For **Waterstones UK**, you'll also need a **NielsenIQ BookData** record (ISBN registration in the UK database).
- For **Indian stores**, consider approaching with a local distributor or consignment offer. **Om Books**, **Rupa**, or **Hachette India** have the widest retail reach.
- For **Powell's Books** specifically: they do not stock Amazon KDP-distributed titles. Make sure distribution is through IngramSpark, not KDP's expanded distribution.

## Draft Outreach Email

**Subject:** Stocking enquiry — *Before the Word* by Praveen Maloo (AI & Sanskrit philosophy)

---

Dear [Buyer's name / Buying Team],

I'm writing to introduce *Before the Word* (paperback, ISBN 979-8349204081), a nonfiction title I believe would resonate strongly with your customers interested in philosophy, technology, and the history of ideas.

**The book in one sentence:** *Before the Word* traces the extraordinary parallel between ancient Sanskrit philosophy and modern artificial intelligence — two traditions separated by twenty-five centuries, both asking the same radical question: what is language before it becomes sound?

The book draws on Pāṇini's generative grammar, Bhartṛhari's theory of meaning, and the Vedic concept of Vāk, and shows how these ideas anticipate — with uncanny precision — the architecture of today's large language models. It is written for a general educated reader: no Sanskrit or computer science required.

**Why now:** AI literacy is one of the fastest-growing sections in bookshops globally, and readers are actively looking for titles that situate the technology in broader intellectual and cultural context. *Before the Word* is one of very few books that approaches AI from a classical Indian philosophy perspective — a distinct and timely angle.

**Available to order:** The paperback is available through Barnes & Noble and can be ordered for your shelves via Ingram Content Group. I am happy to discuss consignment, wholesale terms, or a signed author stock if that would help.

I would be delighted to send a review copy, answer any questions, or arrange a call if useful.

With warm regards,
Praveen Maloo
